InnerWeather Privacy Policy

We believe your emotional data belongs to you — and only you.

Overview

The short version

InnerWeather is a personal mood-tracking app. Almost all of your data stays exclusively on your device. The only time any data leaves your device is when you explicitly request an AI-generated insight — and even then, it is sent securely and never stored on our servers.

We do not create user accounts, we do not sell your data, and we do not track your behavior. Your journal is yours.

Data Collection

What we collect and where it lives

The following data is created when you use InnerWeather:

  • Mood scores (0–100) and the date of each entry
  • Weather condition you select for each day
  • Written notes you optionally add to a day
  • Voice recordings you optionally attach to a day
  • App preferences — theme (light/dark), notification time

Stored locally
All of the above is stored only on your device using Android's private app storage. It is never uploaded to a database or cloud storage service. No one — including us — can access it remotely.

AI Insights

When data temporarily leaves your device

InnerWeather offers optional AI-generated weekly and monthly mood summaries. This feature is entirely opt-in — data is only sent when you tap the "Weekly Insight" or "Monthly Insight" button.

Sent on demand
When you request an insight, the app sends your recent mood entries (scores, dates, weather, and notes — no voice recordings) over an encrypted HTTPS connection to our secure Google Cloud Function. That data is used solely to generate your summary and is not logged, stored, or retained on our servers after the response is returned.

The summary is generated using the OpenAI API. Your mood data is transmitted to OpenAI as part of this process under their Privacy Policy. OpenAI does not use API-submitted data to train their models by default.

The generated summary is cached locally on your device to avoid unnecessary repeat requests. It is never stored on our servers.

Authentication

Firebase Anonymous Authentication

Third-party service
InnerWeather uses Firebase Anonymous Authentication (by Google) solely to authorize requests to our Cloud Function. This creates a temporary, completely anonymous session — no email address, name, phone number, or any personal information is collected or associated with it.

The anonymous session token is used only to verify that a request comes from a legitimate app installation. It is not linked to your identity in any way. When you uninstall the app, the token becomes permanently inactive.

Firebase may collect limited technical data (such as IP address) as part of its service. See Google Firebase Privacy Policy.

Advertising

Google AdMob

Third-party service
InnerWeather displays ads provided by Google AdMob. Google may use advertising identifiers and other data on your device to serve personalized or non-personalized ads.

Your mood data, notes, and voice recordings are never shared with Google AdMob. Ads are shown independently of your journal content.

You can opt out of personalized advertising at any time through your Android device settings: Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalization.

See Google Privacy Policy for details on how Google handles advertising data.

Notifications

Local push notifications

Daily reminder notifications are scheduled and delivered entirely on your device using Android's local notification system. No notification service, push server, or external service is used. Your notification preference and time are stored locally and never transmitted.

Data Retention & Deletion

Your data, your control

All data stored by InnerWeather lives in your device's private app storage. When you uninstall InnerWeather, all your data is permanently deleted by the Android operating system — mood entries, notes, voice recordings, and preferences.

You can also export a full backup of your data at any time from the Settings screen (JSON format), and import it again later if needed.

We do not maintain any server-side database of user data to delete on your behalf, because we never store it in the first place.

Children's Privacy

Under 13

InnerWeather is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 is using the app, please contact us and we will take appropriate steps.
